Research Overview

Disruptive mutations in the gene CHD8 (Chromodomain Helicase DNA-binding protein 8) are highly associated with risk of autism spectrum disorder (ASD)â€”more than a dozen damaging mutations have been identified in CHD8 from unrelated individuals diagnosed with ASD. CHD8 changes DNA accessibility of gene promoters or transcription factor binding sites (TFBS) by binding to histone 3 lysine 4 (H3K4) methyl groups and remodeling nucleosomes. Additionally, CHD8 regulates expression of ASD risk-associated networks during neurodevelopment, and disruption of CHD8 function can alter expression of the genes in these networks. The CHD8 target genes involved in these networks have been identified via high-throughput sequencing in cross species samples such as human neural stem cells, human mid-fetal brain, and embryonic mouse cortex. While CHD8 function has been largely studied in the cortex, its role in other brain structures has been largely unexplored. Our research aims to characterize the molecular and circuit-level changes associated with loss of CHD8 function. Specifically, we are interested to understand how loss of CHD8 affects genes, cell types, and regulatory networks critical for neurodevelopment.

Chd8 binds to promoter regions

Our previous research found increased Chd8 expression in mature neurons of the developing mouse cortex. Which specific cell types show increased Chd8 expression? And what developmental windows are critical for Chd8 function? We are employing snRNAseq and snATACseq analysis in embryonic and postnatal developmental timepoints using using a Chd8+/- mouse line to determine the genome-wide expression and epigenetic profiles that define distinct brain cell populations.

Chd8 regulates expression of many genes

Which genes are regulated by Chd8 in specific cell types? We have generated a transgenic Chd8 Avi-tagged mouse line that allows for specific cell-type biotinylation by a conditionally activated biotin ligase (BirA). We are peforming bioChIP-seq in tissues expressing activated BirA in all cell types and in specific cell-types using Cre driver lines. This will generate chromatin maps of distinct cell types to assess genetic pathways regulated by Chd8.

CHD8 function in human neural stem cells, a model of early neurodevelopment

What are negative and positive regulators of CHD8 expression? Using fluorescent-tagged cell lines and FACseq, we will determine factors that affect CHD8 expression in-vitro.

A long-term consequence of Chd8 haploinsufficiency: Spontaneous seizures in Chd8+/- mice

Some patients with ASD who carry a CHD8 mutation have a history of seizures or abnormal EEG, but this interaction is not well characterized in humans or model systems. Seizure susceptibility increases with age in some Chd8+/- mice. Additionally, male mice have higher seizure incidence than females. These seizures in mice are characterized by behaviors such as immobility, drooling, myoclonic jerks, and Straub tail sign. What is the brain region/circuit responsible for these seizures? Moreover, what are the sex-specific differences? Identifying the abnormal brain activity in our Chd8+/- mice can help with understanding the etiology of the seizure phenotype seen in human patients with damaging CHD8 mutations.
