Research Overview

Disruptive mutations in the gene CHD8 (Chromodomain Helicase DNA-binding protein 8) are highly associated with a diagnosis of autism spectrum disorder (ASD) and other neurodevelopmental disorders. These changes often result in the loss of one copy of CHD8, so that there is less CHD8 protein expression in developing tissues such as the neocortex.

CHD8 binds to histone 3 lysine 4 (H3K4) methyl groups and remodels (or moves) nucleosomes along the DNA. By changing the accessibility of genomic regions, important regulatory regions may be revealed, such as transcription factor binding sites (TFBS) at promoter sites. We have performed chromatin-immunopreciptation assays to determine the localization of CHD8 in neural stem cells, mouse cortex, and prenatal human brain, and find that CHD8 binds most often to promoter regions of active genes.

Additionally, we found that CHD8 regulates the expression of other ASD risk-associated genes during neurodevelopment, and that these genes are co-expressed in ASD risk-associated co-expression networks. Disruption of CHD8 function can therefore alter the expression of the genes in these networks. Our research aims to characterize the molecular and circuit-level changes associated with loss of CHD8 function. Specifically, we seek to understand how loss of CHD8 affects genes, cell types, and regulatory networks critical for neurodevelopment.

Chd8 binds to promoter regions

Our previous research found increased Chd8 expression in mature neurons of the developing mouse cortex. Which specific cell types show increased Chd8 expression? And what developmental windows are critical for Chd8 function? We are employing snRNAseq and snATACseq analysis in embryonic and postnatal developmental timepoints using using a Chd8+/- mouse line to determine the genome-wide expression and epigenetic profiles that define distinct brain cell populations.

Chd8 regulates the expression of many genes

Which genes are regulated by Chd8 in specific cell types? We have generated a transgenic Chd8 Avi-tagged mouse line that allows for specific cell-type biotinylation by a conditionally activated biotin ligase (BirA). We are peforming bioChIP-seq in tissues expressing activated BirA in all cell-types and in specific cell-types using Cre driver lines. This will generate chromatin maps of distinct cell types to assess genetic pathways regulated by Chd8.

CHD8 function in human neural stem cells, a model of early neurodevelopment

What are negative and positive regulators of CHD8 expression? We are studying the effects of environmental factors such as hormones and pesticides on CHD8 expression in-vitro by using an mCherry-tagged CHD8 reporter in hNSCs and employing FACseq.

A long-term consequence of Chd8 haploinsufficiency: Spontaneous seizures in Chd8+/- mice

Some human patients with ASD who carry a CHD8 mutation have a history of seizures or abnormal EEG, but this interaction is not well characterized in humans or model systems. Seizure susceptibility increases with age in some Chd8+/- mice. Additionally, male mice have higher seizure incidence than females. These seizures in mice are characterized by behaviors such as immobility, staring spells, myoclonic jerks, and Straub tail sign. What is the brain region/circuit responsible for these seizures? Moreover, what are the sex-specific differences? Identifying the abnormal brain activity in our Chd8+/- mice, reproducibly measured with vEEG, can help with understanding the etiology of the seizure phenotype seen in human patients with damaging CHD8 mutations.
