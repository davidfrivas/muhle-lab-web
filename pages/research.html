Research

Research Overview

Disruptive mutations in the gene CHD8 (Chromodomain Helicase DNA-binding protein 8) are highly associated with risk of autism spectrum disorder (ASD). CHD8 affects DNA accessibility of gene promoters by remodeling chromatin. Additionally, CHD8 regulates expression of ASD risk-associated networks, and disruption of CHD8 function can alter expression of the genes in these networks. While CHD8 function has been largely studied in the cortex, its role in other brain structures, namely the basal ganglia, has been largely unexplored. Our research aims to characterize the molecular and circuit-level changes associated with loss of CHD8 function. Specifically, we are interested to understand how loss of CHD8 affects the genes, cell types, and regulatory networks critical for dopamine (DA) function in the basal ganglia.
